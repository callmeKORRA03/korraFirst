FUN FACT ABOUT ALPINE ASCENTS

HISTORY

People from been scaling mountain for as a hobby since as early as 1336. Sir Alfred Wills ascent of the Swiss Wetterhorn in 1865 is considerd to be the birth of mountaineering as a sport. Mountaineering is a lifelong philosophy rather than a mere hobby

TYPES & STYLE

There are two main style of mountaineering: Alpine and Expedition. Alpine mountaineering is praticed in medium sized mountains, as opposed to the expedition style's larger mountain. Alpine mountaineer: pack light and move quick and they make a swift push to the peak. Expedition Mountaineer: Move slow and carry much heavier loads.

TECHNIQUE

Technique include face climbing, friction climbing, mantling, use of counterforce (stemming, lie backing and undercling), crack climbing, chimney climbing and down climbing

SHELTRING

Mountain climbing is a strenuous and often dangerous activity, because the weather conditions on the mountain can change without notice, Sheltring can play a critical role in keeping climbers safe and protected . They include: Base Camp,Hut,Tent, Snow Cave,Bivouac.

HAZARD

In term of objective hazard, the dangers mountaineers face include falling rocks, falling ice, snow-avalanches, the climber falling, falls from ice slopes, falls form down snow slopes, falls into crevasses, and the danger from attitude and weather.

GUIDLINES & SAFETY TIPS

(1) Climbing within your ablity level
(2) Know your route.
(3) Make sure your group has a clear communication term and know when to use them.
(4) Always wear helmet.
(5) Check weather before climbing.
